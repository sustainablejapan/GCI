The Weighted Commitment Delivery Index (WCDI)

The **WCDI** is designed to provide a statistically rigorous and democratically representative measure of governmental accountability regarding electoral promises. It addresses the core flaw in traditional promise tracking: treating all pledges equally, regardless of their perceived importance.

The Formula

WCDI = ∑ (Sj × Wi)
∑ Wi × 100

Where **Sj** is the Status Score (0.00 to 1.00) and **Wi** is the Importance Weight (0.5 to 3.0).

Pledge Status Scoring (Sj)

Scores are assigned based on objective outcomes verified by non-partisan fact-checking and legislative data.

Status CategoryScore (Sj)Rationale
Kept / Achieved1.00The promise has been fully delivered as stated.
Strong Compromise / On Track0.75Largely delivered, or on a clear, irreversible path to full completion.
Weak Compromise / In Progress0.50Partial fulfillment achieved; significant progress made, but ultimate success is uncertain.
Stalled / Wait & See / Unclear0.25No substantive action taken; progress is halted or status is unclear.
Broken / Not Kept / Abandoned0.00Explicit failure, reversal of policy, or complete lack of action.

Voter Importance Weighting (Wi)

Weights are derived from normalized, aggregated national polling data regarding the **most important issues** cited by the public during the relevant election cycle. This ensures the index prioritizes the areas the voters mandated.

Policy AreaWeight (Wi)Example Global Pledges
Economy / Health / Immigration3.0Major tax changes, **reducing public healthcare waiting times**, border wall construction or control targets.
Crime / Education / Justice2.0Police recruitment targets, **increasing school funding per student**, sentencing reform.
Environment / Housing / Defence1.0Net-zero targets, **affordable housing construction rates**, defense spending goals.
Minor / Governance / Technical0.5Specific cultural grants, bureaucratic changes, legislative housekeeping.

Crisis Adjustment Factor

The **WCDI-Crisis-Adjusted** score is calculated by excluding pledges whose fulfillment status was demonstrably and severely impacted by major exogenous shocks (e.g., global pandemics, major economic collapses, or wars). These pledges are flagged with a **(C)**.

This provides a cleaner measure of a government’s performance on its core, domestic, and non-crisis-related electoral mandate.
